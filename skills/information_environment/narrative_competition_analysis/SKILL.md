# Narrative Competition Analysis

Narrative Competition Analysis examines how rival narratives about the same event, actor, or issue compete for attention, belief, and durability in an information environment. Drawing on framing theory, meme-competition models, and computational narrative analysis, the technique identifies each narrative's core claim, emotional valence, target audience, rhetorical devices, and propagation channels, then assesses relative salience, resilience, and vulnerability to counter-narrative intervention. It is a defensive analytical tool for understanding the landscape before designing protective communications or resilience strategies.

## When to use

- Assessing the information environment before a planned public communication to understand what narratives the message will compete with
- Evaluating why a true and well-sourced narrative is losing salience to a false but emotionally resonant competitor
- Mapping adversarial narrative strategies to identify rhetorical pressure points and counter-narrative opportunities
- Monitoring narrative dynamics during a fast-moving crisis or information operation
- Designing resilience communications that inoculate audiences against anticipated false narratives

## What it produces

- A structured map of competing narratives with core claims, frames, audiences, and channels
- Relative salience estimates showing which narrative currently commands more attention and why
- Rhetorical analysis explaining each narrative's emotional and cognitive appeals
- Identified vulnerabilities in each narrative — factual weaknesses, logical gaps, or audience mismatches — that can anchor counter-narrative strategy

## Key discipline

- Narratives compete on emotional resonance and simplicity, not accuracy alone — a technically correct counter-narrative loses if it is emotionally inert
- Frame early: the first credible narrative to occupy cognitive space has a structural advantage (illusory truth effect, continued influence effect)
- Assess durability, not just current salience — a narrative's ability to survive debunking is a separate dimension from its current spread
- Identify the narrative's core emotional appeal before designing any counter-measure; attacking the facts while ignoring the emotional frame rarely works
