# Research Methods for Analysis

Research methods for analysis are the structured practices for gathering, grading, and synthesizing evidence and for expressing judgments with calibrated, communicable confidence.

## Why it matters for cognitive security

An analytic product is only as trustworthy as the method behind it. Ungraded evidence, uncalibrated confidence, and vague language let bias and adversarial framing slip through unchecked. Structured synthesis and calibrated estimation make analysis reproducible and its uncertainty honest — so decision-makers know not just *what* is claimed but *how strongly* and *on what basis*.

## Core concepts

- **Structured literature & evidence synthesis** — systematic, documented review of sources (search strategy, inclusion criteria, deduplication) rather than convenience sampling.
- **Evidence grading** — the **Admiralty Code / NATO source-reliability and information-credibility scale** (source A–F × info 1–6) for intelligence, and **GRADE** (high/moderate/low/very-low certainty) for scientific evidence.
- **Calibrated estimation** — Tetlock's superforecasting findings: probabilistic, granular estimates; frequent updating; track-record scoring (Brier scores); and the value of breaking questions into tractable parts.
- **Analytic confidence** — expressing confidence as a separate dimension from likelihood (e.g., high/moderate/low confidence based on source quality, corroboration, and assumption strength), per intelligence-community standards.
- **BLUF reporting** — Bottom Line Up Front structure: the key judgment first, then supporting evidence and caveats, so conclusions and their uncertainty are unmistakable.
- **Estimative language** — standardized probability words (e.g., "likely," "probable") mapped to ranges to avoid ambiguous verbal hedging.

## How CogSecSkills operationalizes this

Skills in this group encode these as repeatable agentic steps: tagging sources with Admiralty/GRADE ratings, structuring a synthesis with an audit trail, eliciting calibrated probability estimates with explicit confidence, and formatting findings in BLUF. Each yields a structured, reviewable product, never an unsourced assertion.

## Defensive & ethical framing

These methods serve transparency and accountability — every judgment carries its evidence grade, confidence, and assumptions so it can be checked and challenged. Use is bounded by intellectual honesty and human oversight, not advocacy or persuasion.
