# Media Literacy Assessment

Media Literacy Assessment evaluates an audience's capacity to critically appraise information sources, distinguishing genuine content from manipulated or misleading material. It applies lateral-reading and verification heuristics drawn from news literacy research (SIFT, CRAAP) to diagnose specific gaps in an audience's epistemic habits. The output is an actionable gap map that prioritizes which verification competencies to strengthen and with what educational interventions.

## When to use

- designing or evaluating a media-literacy curriculum for a specific community
- assessing an organization's or population's susceptibility to influence operations before a high-stakes information event
- diagnosing why a particular misinformation narrative spread successfully through an audience
- benchmarking progress after a literacy-training intervention

## What it produces

- a scored competency gap map across lateral reading, source evaluation, verification habit, and emotional override dimensions
- ranked intervention recommendations tied to specific identified gaps, with expected efficacy notes

See [`workflow.md`](workflow.md). Harness bindings in [`harness/`](harness/).

## Key discipline

- use lateral-reading as the primary behavioral benchmark — does the audience leave a site to investigate it, not just read deeper into it
- distinguish between declarative knowledge (knowing a checklist) and behavioral habit (actually applying it under cognitive load)
- prioritize emotional override resistance — high-arousal content bypasses declarative literacy knowledge even in trained audiences
