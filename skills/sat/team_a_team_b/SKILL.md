# Team A / Team B

Team A / Team B is a structured competitive-analysis technique in which two teams develop and defend competing interpretations of the same body of evidence, then engage in structured debate so that decision-makers can observe the strongest available case for each view. Unlike a devil's advocate exercise where a single critic challenges a consensus, Team A/B requires each team to advocate its own hypothesis with full conviction, surfacing the genuine strongest version of each competing explanation. The technique counteracts groupthink, premature consensus, and the tendency of a dominant faction to suppress minority views.

## When to use

- two or more substantially different explanations of the same evidence are genuinely plausible and the analytic community has begun consolidating prematurely around one
- a high-stakes decision depends on which of two competing interpretations is correct and the cost of being wrong is asymmetric
- groupthink or hierarchical deference is suspected of suppressing a minority analytic view
- a previous assessment was wrong and the post-mortem suggests an alternative hypothesis was not given full evidentiary development
- a consumer or policymaker has requested explicit treatment of competing views rather than a single-point estimate

## What it produces

- the strongest honest version of each competing hypothesis, developed with full conviction rather than strawmanned
- explicit identification of the key evidence that discriminates between the two hypotheses
- a list of what each team must concede — evidence that undermines its own position
- an explicit assessment of which hypothesis is currently better supported with confidence level and key residual uncertainties

## Key discipline

- each team must argue its hypothesis in good faith with full conviction — this is not devil's advocacy, it is genuine competitive development
- the debate must surface the strongest version of each case, not a strawman of the opposition
- the moderator must force each team to acknowledge the evidence that most undermines its position before the debate closes
- the output is not a compromise position — it is an honest adjudication of which hypothesis is better supported and why
