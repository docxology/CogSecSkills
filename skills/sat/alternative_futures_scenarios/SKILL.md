# Alternative Futures (Scenarios)

Alternative Futures Analysis (Scenarios) constructs a small set of mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive futures defined by the intersection of critical uncertainties, forcing analysts and decision-makers to reason beyond the single most-likely-outcome trap. Rooted in the RAND/Shell scenario planning tradition and codified for intelligence analysis by Heuer & Pherson, the technique stresses strategy against each scenario and surfaces indicators that discriminate between them. It counters anchoring, mirror-imaging, and surprise-aversion by keeping multiple divergent worlds simultaneously in view.

## When to use

- The situation involves high uncertainty across two or more independent dimensions where the outcome could diverge sharply
- Decision-makers need to stress-test a strategy or plan against multiple possible futures rather than optimizing for one
- An existing single-point forecast may be anchoring analysis and suppressing consideration of tail risks
- Early-warning indicators are needed to detect which future is emerging before it is confirmed

## What it produces

- A structured set of 2–4 distinct, plausible futures organized around the most critical uncertainties
- A scenario matrix making the logic of each future transparent and comparable
- A discriminating indicator list enabling real-time tracking of which scenario is unfolding
- An explicit record of strategy vulnerabilities exposed under each alternative world

## Key discipline

- Select uncertainty axes that are genuinely independent and truly uncertain — not disguised probability estimates dressed as scenarios
- Each scenario must be internally coherent and plausible, not merely a best-case / worst-case / status-quo triplet
- Indicators must discriminate between scenarios — a signal that is consistent with all scenarios has no diagnostic value
- The technique's primary value is cognitive: holding multiple futures simultaneously forces the analyst off the single-outcome default
