# Workflow — Outside-In Thinking

Harness-neutral agentic procedure. Each step names the tool verb(s) it uses (see `skill.yaml` → `tools`); a harness adapter binds each verb.

## Step 1 — Read and state the focal issue (read)
Read the focal issue statement and any prior assessments. Record the dominant hypotheses or conclusions already held. These will be revisited after the macro scan to identify what outside forces they failed to account for.

## Step 2 — Populate the PESTLE macro layer (reason, write)
Systematically work through Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, and Environmental dimensions. For each, ask: what forces are active, what is their direction of change, and what is their velocity? Write a brief entry for each that is non-trivial for the focal issue.

## Step 3 — Identify mezzo-level constraints (reason, write)
From the PESTLE inventory, derive the organizational, institutional, and network-level constraints that macro forces impose on actors in the focal situation. These are the transmission mechanisms between macro environment and focal-actor behavior.

## Step 4 — Revisit focal-level analysis with macro frame (reason, write)
Return to the focal issue. For each dominant hypothesis, identify which macro forces support, undermine, or constrain it. Revise or reweight hypotheses accordingly. Note which macro factors are load-bearing for the conclusion and flag them as key assumptions.

## Step 5 — Document exposed assumptions and implications (reason, write)
List the assumptions the original inside-out framing carried that the outside-in scan reveals. Assess each as confirmed, challenged, or uncertain. Produce the final layered analysis with macro forces explicitly linked to focal-level implications.

## Anti-criteria (must NOT happen)
- do not begin writing about the focal actor before the PESTLE macro layer is fully populated — premature actor focus is the bias this technique is designed to break
- do not list PESTLE factors that have no traceable causal path to the focal issue; a superficial scan produces the appearance of rigor without the substance
- do not treat the technique as complete unless at least one prior assumption from inside-out analysis has been explicitly confirmed or challenged
